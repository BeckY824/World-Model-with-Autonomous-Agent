# The Representation Shift of World Models
Driven by recent advances in large language models, autonomous agents have attracted significant attention. However, the absence of a strong physical foundation continues to expose major structural limitations in current systems. While internal world models are widely recognized as a key component for achieving genuine agency, existing research often categorizes these systems by applications rather than by their underlying cognitive architectures.

This repository provides a comprehensive review of the architectural evolution of world models and proposes a unified conceptual framework for understanding how agents represent environmental dynamics. We organize this evolution into three major paradigms:

1. Explicit symbolic world models — human-engineered representations and rule-based mappings.
2. Latent neural world models — experience-driven continuous dynamics learned from interaction.
3. Discrete semantic world models — data-driven semantic abstractions built upon large-scale tokenized representations.

By analyzing the structural transition across these paradigms, this review highlights their respective strengths, limitations, and open challenges, while outlining future directions toward robust and physically grounded artificial intelligence.

本项目深入探讨了智能体架构中世界模型(World Models)的演进，分析了其表征框架如何从早期的显式符号地图转变为现代的潜空间神经动力学，并最终延伸至基于大语言模型（LLM）的智能体。本项目不仅将世界模型视为一种独立理论，更将其作为重新思考智能体进化史的核心视角。

The review paper is on [The Representation Shift of World Models: From
Explicit Symbolic Maps to Latent Neural Dynamics
and LLM-Based Agents](https://github.com/BeckY824/World-Model-with-Autonomous-Agent/blob/main/Views-from-different-timespan/IRP_Final_Report.pdf).

## 🚀 <b>Further Directions & Plan</b><br>
Exploring the fundamental mechanisms of how the brain works through interdisciplinary research combining Machine Learning, Neuroscience, and Cognitive Science. Key focus areas include:<br>
🧠 <b>1. Thinking Module:</b> Modeling reasoning, decision-making, and complex problem-solving processes.<br>
💾 <b>2. Memory Module:</b> Investigating information encoding, storage, and retrieval mechanisms (e.g., working memory vs. long-term memory).<br>
👁️ <b>3. Perception & Attention Module:</b> Understanding how sensory inputs are processed and how cognitive resources are allocated.<br>
🤖 <b>4. Learning & Adaptation Module:</b> Studying neuroplasticity, continuous learning frameworks, and adaptive behaviors.

通过结合机器学习（ML）、神经科学与认知科学的跨学科研究，深入探索大脑的底层工作机制。核心研究模块包括：<br>
🧠 <b>1. 思考模块 (Thinking Module):</b> 对推理、决策以及复杂问题的解决过程进行系统建模。<br>
💾 <b>2. 记忆模块 (Memory Module):</b> 研究信息的编码、存储与检索机制（例如工作记忆与长时记忆的交互）。<br>
👁️ <b>3. 感知与注意力模块 (Perception & Attention Module):</b> 探索大脑如何处理感觉输入以及如何高效分配认知资源。<br>
🤖 <b>4. 学习与适应模块 (Learning & Adaptation Module):</b> 研究神经可塑性、持续学习框架以及动态适应行为。
